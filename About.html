I'm Folkert Debonnet,
Brussels-based creative technologist.

Recently graduated in Multimedia & Creative Technology. I work at the intersection of interactive systems, cognitive design, and emerging media — designing not just how technology works, but how people experience it.

It started at 14 with an Arduino and a single question: "what can I make this do?" That board unlocked something — technology became a material to shape, not just consume. Since then I've built sensors, interactive installations, video productions, and AI-driven workflows.

My Erasmus in Porto added another layer: surrounded by people from radically different backgrounds, I stopped asking "does this work?" and started asking "does this work for this person, in this context?" That cognitive shift is now central to everything I design.

Today I'm building AI agents, experimenting with automation, and obsessing over the design challenge beneath the tech — how do you create a system that truly fits how a human thinks?
